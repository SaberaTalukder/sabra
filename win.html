What is Neuroengineering?

October 15, 2018

    Neuroengineering. Neural engineering. Neurotechnology.

This burgeoning, fuzzily defined, interdisciplinary field of neuroscience, electrical engineering, biochemistry, and computer science has barely begun.

     The field is so young that many researchers do not even agree on joint terminology! Given the field's infancy, in this blog post I hope to demistify some aspects of neuroengineering which will hopefully allow you to more easily approach and understand the field.

     In this blog post we are going to: define neuroengineering, and outline the field's divisions and goals.

    Neuroengineering Definition

     Since neuroengineering is such an interdisciplinary field, you should have a working understanding of neuroscience, electrical engineering, biochemistry, and computer science. Although this is a lot of information, it will well be worth it!

    Neuroengineering is the study of using engineering principles to (1) read and write information out of the brain, (2) interrogate principles of neuroscience systems, or (3) inform the algorithims used in hardware or software systems. Wooh that was a mouthful! Let's break it down.

    The first category of reading and writing information out of the brain is encapsulated by many names such as neural prostheses, brain machine interfaces (BMIs), neuroprosthetics, and brain computer interfaces (BCIs).

    The second categroy of interrogating principles of neuroscience systems is largely dominated by the phenomenon of optogenetics. Which is a way of inducing or suppressing neurons to fire with light. Please refesh on the tool if you are not familiar. Optogenetics, has been a revolutionary tool for the field of neuroengineering and neuroscience because it allows you to perturb neural systems in more causal manner than was ever possible before. Also calcium and voltage indicators, but that is mostly for observation, not as much as control which is what optogenetics is.

    The third an final category is using the algorithms and principles that organize the nervous systems to create better hardware and software. This can largely contains neuromorphic computing which is the hardware component, and software component which is more realistic and competent artificial intelligence systems.

    Goal's of Neuroengineering

    The goals of each of these subdivisions of neuroengineering are as follows:

    The goals of 1 are possibly the most split in the field right now. And are to help people that are suffering from paralyasis, depression, parkinsons, etc. or to augment the human mind. *Cough* *Cough* if it was not obvious that I was talking about Elon I was.

    The goals of optogenetics, as well as the other ways to control neurons magnetogenetics anyone? are to be able to more causally perturb the neural system we are interested in in response to a certain stimulus or behavior. This has proved to be cruicial for many learning and memory experiments/ The goals of calcium and voltage inicators are to be able to observe the system that is active during a particular stimulus or behavior, however with these methods you cannot perturb - only observe.

    THe goals of borrowing neuroscience principles to inform hardware and software creations is to make more competent machines and algorithms that can perform more complex compuations and tasks. The fields of neuromorphic computing as well as artificial intelligence direly want to borrow from neuroscience so that we can eventually have consumer owned self-driving cars, or more complex robotic assistants.

    In future blog posts we will dive into each of these components more in depth, as well as walk through who the main players are in the fields both in academia as well as industry, as well as talk about what the most compelling technology is in each space.

    But that is for another blog post! In short neuroengineering is a vast space that pulls all the way from biochemistry to machine learning. It is an exciting time for neuroengineering as things are ramping up, and stay tuned for more updates in the space!
