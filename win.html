What is Neuroengineering?

October 15, 2018

    Neuroengineering. Neural engineering. Neurotechnology.

This burgeoning, fuzzily defined, interdisciplinary field of neuroscience, electrical engineering, biochemistry, and computer science has barely begun.

     The field is so young that many researchers do not even agree on joint terminology! Given the field's infancy, in this blog post I hope to demistify some aspects of neuroengineering, which will hopefully allow you to more easily approach and understand the field.
     In this blog post we are going to: define neuroengineering, and outline the divisions of research within the field. Sounds simple enough! ðŸ˜ƒ

    Neuroengineering Definition

     Since neuroengineering is such an interdisciplinary field, you should have a working understanding of neuroscience, electrical engineering, biochemistry, and computer science before jumping in. A wikipedia refresher should do just fine! ðŸ˜Š

     Neuroengineering is the study of appling engineering principles to one of following categories:
    • Reading and writing information out of the brain
    • Interrogating biological principles of neuroscience systems
    • Informing the algorithims used in hardware or software designs

     Wooh that was a mouthful! Let's break it down.

     Reading and writing information out of the brain is largely encapsulated in the field of neuroprosthetics. The devices created within the neuroprosthetics field go by many names such as brain machine interfaces (BMIs), neural prostheses, and brain computer interfaces (BCIs). These terms are mostly interchangeable.

     Any hardware or software created to collect, compile, or decipher information from the brain would be a neuroengineering system that fits into this category. An awesome example is the neuropixel probe that can record from hundreds of neurons across multiple brain regions. The neuropixel is pictured below; the red arrows indicated the piece of the probe that is inserted into the brain ðŸ˜±. The picture is incredibly faint, because the probe has a diameter of only seventy micrometers!
     An important note: some devices that neither read nor write infomation out of the brain, like deep brain stimulators (DBS) for Parkinson's Disease, are sometimes referred to as neural prostheses. They are more appropriately characterized as neuromodulatory devices, regardless, they are still incredibly effective at treating some diseases!

     Interrogating biological principles of neuroscience systems is largely dominated by the phenomenon of optogenetics. Optogenetics is a method to induce or suppress neural activity by making neurons responsive to specific wavelengths of light. I swear this is not fiction, just incredible science! ðŸ”¬

     Pictured below is a mouse with an optical fiber implanted in its head than can trigger optically responsive neurons, or in other words, what I imagine is one of the most famous mice in the world!
     Optogenetics, has been a revolutionary tool for the field of neuroengineering and neuroscience because it allows you to perturb neural systems in more causal manner than was ever possible before.

     Informing the algorithims used in hardware or software designs is another field of neuroengineering that, you guessed it, aims to create more capable and efficient hardware and software by deriving inspiration from neuroscience.

     An example of neuroengineering in hardware is neuromorphic computing. Neuromorphic chips are hardware that utilize both analog and digital components to more realistically simulate the nervous system. Pictured below is me giving a presentation on my research with Neurogrid, a type of neuromorphic chip.
     On the software side: aritificial intelligence algorithms, such as Neural Networks and Deep Q Networks, have heavily borrowed principles from neuroscience. The principles borrowed have led to dramatically improved performance on tasks such as Atari! ðŸ˜±

     The sharing between the two field has even been dubbed a virtuous circle. Thus far, however, information has flown mostly in one direction!

     Exceptions to our categories. Within this article I have alluded to the presence of other devices and fields within neuroengineering, like neuromodulatory devices (i.e. DBSs). By no means have we explored everything created or existing in the field of neuroengineering. For example, I am intentionally leaving out non-invasive neuroengineering therapies. However, the technologies listed here are some of most promising and rigorous in the field.

     Perhaps in the future we will explore the history of neuroengineering, and everything under the sun that it encompasses! ðŸ˜Š

    Final Thoughts

      Neuroengineering is a dynamic and highly interdisciplinary field that aims to understand, mimic, control, and ultimately master the brain. Needless to say, it is an exciting time!
